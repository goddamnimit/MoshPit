_Last updated: July 6, 2026_

MoshPit is a real-time video effects app. The short version: **we collect
nothing.**

## What we collect

Nothing. MoshPit has no analytics, no crash reporting service, no ads, no
account system, and no servers. The app never sends your video, audio,
usage data, or anything else to us or to anyone — we have no way to receive
it.

## Camera and microphone

The camera is used as a live video source for the effects you see on screen.
The microphone is used only while you record a clip, so your recording has
sound. All processing happens on your device. No camera or microphone data
ever leaves your device unless you yourself turn on a network output (see
below).

## Photo library

If you choose a video from your library, MoshPit reads that video to use it
as an effect source. When you record a clip or save a snapshot, MoshPit adds
it to your library. That's the full extent of photo library access.

## Local network (NDI and MJPEG output)

MoshPit can stream its video output over your local Wi-Fi network to
software you run yourself, such as VJ or streaming tools, using the NDI
protocol or a built-in MJPEG web stream. This only happens when you switch
an output on, and the video goes only to devices on your own network that
you point at the stream. MoshPit never connects to the internet on its own.

## Third-party components

MoshPit includes the NDI SDK (NewTek/Vizrt) solely to encode and send video
on your local network when you enable NDI output. It performs no analytics
or tracking in MoshPit. There are no other third-party SDKs.

## Settings stored on your device

Your preferences (effect order, MIDI mappings, whether you've seen the
tutorial) are stored locally on your device and sync nowhere.

## Children

MoshPit collects no data from anyone, including children.

## Changes

If a future version ever changes any of the above, this policy will be
updated and the change will be called out in the App Store release notes.
